THE CHALLENGE

Traditional olive farming leaves too much to chance

Scattered field records, reactive pest response, and climate uncertainty make olive production harder to predict. Today’s olive sector needs earlier signals, clearer risk visibility, and better decisions.

See How Viora Solves This
The Problem Cards
01

Olive trees naturally alternate between high and low production years. Without precise stress management and data tracking, financial forecasting and harvest planning become incredibly difficult.

02

Pests like the Olive Fruit Fly can devastate crops rapidly. Relying on reactive treatments instead of early alerts leads to irreversible crop loss, lower oil quality, and excessive chemical costs.

03

The El Niño-Southern Oscillation drastically alters temperature and humidity. These sudden micro-climate shifts directly disrupt flowering, fruit set, and overall orchard health.

Olive farming is becoming more volatile. Don’t just react to field risk — anticipate it. Viora helps you move forward with actionable data, earlier pest-risk visibility, and direct access to the right crop protection specialist.
